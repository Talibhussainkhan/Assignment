Qeema

Keema is a South Asian dish of minced meat in an aromatic sauce of spices and herbs: Origin Keema is thought to have originated in the northern regions of India, Pakistan, and Bangladesh. It's traditionally a Mughal dish.

Ingredients

Keema is typically made with minced meat like beef, lamb, mutton, or chicken, along with onions, tomatoes, and spices like star anise, cloves, cinnamon, curry powder, and chili powder. Other ingredients may include peas, potatoes, yogurt, and herbs.

>Preparation

Keema is often cooked in a pan with oil or butter, where the spices are fried until aromatic, then the onions are cooked until soft and browned. The meat is then added and cooked un til it releases its juices, and the dish is finished with herbs and other ingredients.
