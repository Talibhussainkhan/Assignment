Parathas

Paratha is a flaky, unleavened, layered flatbread that originated in South Asia and is commonly made in India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, and Nepal:

Ingredients

Made from flour, water, salt, oil, and ghee

Preparation

The dough is rolled out thinly, coated with oil or ghee, folded back and forth, and then rolled into a round shape. The paratha is then baked or shallow-fried on a griddle or tava

Variations

Parathas can be plain or stuffed with a variety of ingredients, such as potatoes, lentils, or minced meat. Stuffed parathas are often prepared in different styles depending on the region of origin.
